For Land Owners

Whether you just bought your first 10 acres or your family has held land for generations, we help you turn “a piece of woods” into a working, well-planned property.

Dialed-in habitat, not random projects.

A single food plot or a quick cut of timber rarely fixes anything by itself. We look at the whole property — access, wind, cover, food, timber, and water — and design work that actually moves the needle.

  • Hunt-focused layouts that still make sense year-round
  • Clear priorities if money or time is limited
  • Options for doing work yourself vs. hiring it out

Typical landowner projects

  • First-time walk-through and “where do I start?” plan
  • Reworking an over-hunted or over-cut property
  • Balancing timber income with habitat improvements

For Contractors

Need someone who speaks both “plans and specs” and “boots in the mud”? 23 Timber Co. partners with contractors on land-heavy projects across New Hampshire and northern New England.

Site & Access Layout

Help with layout for access roads, landings, staging areas, and equipment paths that respect terrain, soils, and landowner constraints.

Clearing & Habitat-Sensitive Work

Plans that balance what the project needs with what the landowner wants left standing – especially on cabins, camps, and rural builds.

Owner-Facing Support

Walk the ground with the client, explain tradeoffs in plain English, and help set expectations so your crews can just get to work.

Why contractors bring us in

  • Single point of contact who understands landowners and field crews
  • Maps and notes that slot into your existing process
  • Helps reduce change orders and “while you’re here” scope creep

About 23 Timber Co.

23 Timber Co. exists for people who see their land as more than a tax bill or a logging ticket. If you care about wildlife, timber, and what your place looks like in 20+ years, you’re in the right spot.

The work blends practical forestry, habitat design, and real hunting experience on New England ground. The goal is simple: help you make better decisions with your acres.

About the owner

I grew up working in the woods, around equipment, and on small farms. Today, I split my time between walking land, planning projects, and helping owners figure out “what should we actually do first?”

I’m not interested in drive-by consulting or selling you projects you don’t need. The work is built around realistic budgets, honest trade-offs, and plans you can actually execute.
