How & Why 23 Timber Co. Started

Legacy • Stewardship • Origin Story

Most companies in this industry start with equipment.
23 Timber Co. started with a question:

“What would the woods look like if people managed their land the way the land actually needs to be managed?”

For years, I watched New England woodlots decline — not from neglect, but from misunderstanding. Land that *should’ve* been producing wildlife, timber value, clean water, and generational memory instead became overgrown, unhealthy, or randomly cut without strategy.

At the same time, I kept having the same conversation with landowners:
They didn’t want a logging job. They didn’t want a landscaping job.
They wanted a plan.

A plan that respected:

  • Wildlife habitat
  • Timber value and rotation
  • Access, trails, and water
  • Fire behavior and risk reduction
  • The next generation — not just the next 12 months

But the industry is split: loggers focus on timber, conservation groups focus on ecology, hunters focus on wildlife… rarely do they overlap.

23 Timber Co. exists to sit directly in the middle of all three.

Where it really began

I grew up around land, machinery, and woods that told stories — but I didn’t fully appreciate how deep that connection ran until adulthood. Years of reading, working, burning, planting, learning, and observing slowly matured into a single mission:

“Build land plans that outlive their owners.”

Not plans that look good on paper. Plans that hold up in storms, droughts, winters, markets, wildlife pressure, time, and generations.

That’s when it clicked: every acre deserves intentional design — the same way a home, business, or legacy does.

Why “23”?

More than anything, the name represents:

  • Returning to the land
  • Building something that lasts
  • A personal mark for my kids to inherit someday

It’s a reminder that the work isn’t just about today — it’s about the generation that follows, and the land they’ll stand on.

The mission going forward

23 Timber Co. will always be built around three pillars:

  • Habitat — design that supports wildlife
  • Timber — long-term economic value
  • Legacy — something worth passing down

This company exists because the land deserves better — and because the people who own it deserve clarity, confidence, and partnership.

If we do our job right, the woods will show it long after we’re gone.
